Adult Enrollment

We're excited to have you join us! By registering for our program, you are agreeing to the following:

Terms

1. Season Commitment

Registration is for a full 4-month season. This helps us build consistency, develop skills, and create a strong training environment. By enrolling, you are committing to the full season, regardless of attendance or changes in your availability.

2. Roles & Expectations

Our role is to provide a great coaching experience, thoughtful programming, and an environment where people can train hard, develop their skills, and improve, with clear expectations around participation, hygiene, and conduct.


To get the most out of this program and become good training partners, members are encouraged to train regularly and attend as often as they're physically able and their personal commitments allow.


All members are expected to:

  • Try to be good training partners
  • Make every reasonable effort not to hurt their training partners
  • Welcome and support newer members

3. How to Be Successful in Training

Jiu-jitsu can be challenging at times. Progress isn't always linear, and there will be periods when training feels difficult or motivation dips.


We encourage participants to approach those moments with patience and perseverance rather than stepping away at the first sign of difficulty. Consistency over time builds skill, confidence, and long-term success, leading to a rewarding experience.

4. Payment

The total program fee is $595 + GST for the 4-month season. Full payment is required at the time of registration to secure your spot. Spaces are limited and are filled on a first-come, first-served basis.

5. Refunds & Withdrawals

All registrations are final. As your spot is reserved for the full season, we do not offer refunds, credits, or transfers for missed classes, withdrawals, changes in availability, or injury or illness.


This allows us to keep classes consistent and plan staffing and programming properly.


If you're unable to train due to injury or illness, we will support a safe return to training when you're ready.

6. Health & Hygiene

To help prevent skin infections and keep everyone healthy, members agree to:

  • Check regularly for skin issues (redness, irritation, itchiness, bumps, or ring-shaped marks) and refrain from training if symptoms are present
  • Shower promptly after class
  • Wear clean training clothes to each practice
  • Cover any cuts or scrapes before class
  • Keep fingernails and toenails trimmed short
